Limitation of BMI

Although BMI is often a practical indicator of healthy weight, it is not suited for every person. specific groups should carefully consider their BMI outcomes, and in certain cases, the measurement may not be benificial to use.

Gender

The development and body fat composition of girls and boys vary with age. Consequently, a child's age and gender are considered when evaluating their BMI

Age

In aging individuals, increased body fat and muscle loss ma cause BMI to underestimate body fat content

Muscle

BMI may missclassify muscular individuals as overweight or obese as it doesn't differentiate muscle from fat

Pregnancy

Expectant mothers experience weight gain due to their growing baby. Maintaining a healthy pre-pregnancy BMI is advisible to minimise health risks for both mother and child

Race

Certain health concerns affect individuals of some Black and Asian origins at lower BMI's than others. To learn more, it is advised to discuss with your GP or practice nurse
